Industry Engagement in Congress
What positions on legislation did diffent groups most often take

The below visualization shows positions taken by organizations on bills from the 112th congress that came up for a vote in at least one chamber. The theory is, by looking at the number of bills supported / opposed by a given organization, we can estimate their level of engagement and gauge whether events in congress are working in or against their favor. By focusing on bills that made it all the way to a vote, hopefully we're filtering out bills introduced as symbolic gestures or used for political grandstanding.

The data for this visualization comes from Maplight and the Center for Responsive Politics. The CRP, as part of its Open Data Initiative, compiles and releases large bungles of campaign finance data. Maplight pulls in that data and references the contributions by different organizations while painstakingly cataloging those organizations support and opposition to versions of proposed bills.
